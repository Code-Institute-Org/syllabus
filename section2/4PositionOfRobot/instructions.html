Instructions

Situation and Goal

In the first part of this exercise, you will learn to control a Thymio robot in position, speed and force.

Then, you will take into account the values returned by the distance sensors of the Thymio to make your robot avoid the obstacle automatically

What is already done?

All the initializations is already done: the front distance sensors are enable and we retrieved the motors for you and put them in variables.

What is left to do?

This time, you will program the whole behaviour of the robot. To begin, you will activate the engine and then link their speed with the value of the distance sensors.

Hands-on

1 Control one motor in position

Explanation

First, you wil control the motor of a wheel in position, exactly as you did in the previous exercises. And to really begin with the simplest thing, you will activate only motor, the leftMotor

Task

  • Have a look at the simulation, in the simulation window. If you closed it, you can reopen it by clicking the 3D cube icon.
  • Open the controller by going in the code editor(IDE)->File->Open... and open the robot_controller.py file.

Expected result

The Thymio robot has a lot of distance sensors: 5 in the front, 2 in the back and two below, towards the ground. But for this exercise, we will use only the front sensors. If your robot touches obstacles: - decrease speed - adjust the calibration constant - calibrate how much your robot must turn depending on the values of the sensors What happen if the central distance sensor detects something? If the robot get blocked: - Try to detect it: this Thymio has an embedded GPS. If the value of the GPS does not change => the robot is blocked - Try to understand why it is blocked: look in the robot window to see the values of the distance sensors - Try to fix it: - Go back and turn in a random direction. - Turn on the point when all sensors detects something
